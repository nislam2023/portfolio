Design and fabrication of mid-IR erbium-doped ZBLAN fiber laser operating at 2.7um wavelength

Motivation

ZBLAN is considered as the most stable fluoride glass and an excellent host for rare-earth ions. Hence, it has been studied towards the fabrication of fiber lasers in the mid-infrared. ZBLAN fiber lasers are not only demonstrating promising high-power emissions in the mid-infrared, but are also becoming the preferred ones because of their excellent beam combining techniques, low background loss, and effective heat-dissipation with respect to other lasers. Coupling of high pump power and high allowable doping levels (up to 10 mol %) have made ZBLAN fiber a medium of choice for laser fabrication.The double-clad structure with high numerical aperture allows for efficient pumping of the gain medium

  • ZBLAN stands for ZrF-BaF2-LaF3-AlF3-NaF
  • Low loss transmission between 250nm to 5µm
  • Low phonon energies of 500-600/cm
  • Good heat management
  • Better thermo-optical properties than Er3+dopped crystal

Application

  • Laser Surgery
  • Frequency Conversion
  • Spectroscopy
  • Airplane & Satellite Communication
  • Environmental Monitoring

Result

  • the signal is -23 dBm and the residual pump power is about -33 dBm while the launched pump power is a little higher than 1 W.
  • Ideally, it would have been desirable to filter out the pump from the lasing signal but the dichroic filter (M1) had a pump rejection of 11 dB which is typical for commercially available dichroic filters.
  • The maximum output power at a wavelength of 2.7 μm is measured as -18 dBm (16 μW) from a 4 W incident pump power.
  • This amount of signal power represents the 28% of actual available signal power in the cavity. The ZnSe output coupler(OC) attenuates total power specially the undepleted pump to ensure safe operation of the OSA. Then again, there is 13 dBm loss is associated for out-coupling the light through the patch cord. Therefore, it is estimated that the laser emits a total power of ~-5 dBm or 316 μW.

Design, Fabrication and Characterization of Mach-Zehnder Interferometer

  • MZI designs are described with prosed layout of several MZIs, in the SOI technology, with 220nm thickness and 500nm width waveguides in the strip configuration.
  • Different parameters are compared in a table to understand the characteristics of MZI.
  • the design of an MZI is proposed which contains the following components – fiber grating couplers for guiding light into and out of the interferometer, y-branches for splitting and combining light and strip waveguides for guiding light.
  • In an MZI, phase difference between the input light waves travelling in the two waveguides can be achieved using path difference between the two waveguides, using materials having different effective indices and attenuation constants. In this design, the path length mismatch is used to generate the phase shift.
  • The waveguide is rectangular and has commonly industry-standard width of 500nm and height of 220nm. It also has the first quasi-TE mode profile at 1550nm.
  • There is a spectral red shift of 0.74 nm at higher temperature resulting in a thermal shift of 0.2 nm/°C
  • The optical insertion loss is measured to be 5.4 dB which is an ideal grating coupler loss and the ER is 38 dB at room temperature. The FSR is 2.48 nm.
